L001: In Applicant A v Respondent B, the court applied a three-part test for interim injunctions.
L002: First, the court asked whether there was a serious question to be tried.
L003: Second, the court considered whether damages would be an adequate remedy.
L004: Third, the court assessed the balance of convenience.
L005: The court emphasised that the three factors are not rigid boxes.
L006: The court found a serious question existed on the facts.
L007: The court held damages were not adequate due to irreparable reputational harm.
L008: The court found the balance of convenience favoured the applicant.
L009: The court granted the interim injunction with conditions.
L010: The judgment noted the respondent could apply to vary the order.
